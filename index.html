Washington state is the north-western most state of the continguous United States. The state is often known for its mountains, pine trees, rain, and the city of Seattle, which is the largest city in that part of the country. These features, however, are largely exclusive to the western side of the state. The eastern region is vastly different. Dominated by the Columbia River Basin, most of eastern washington could be considered a semi-destern or shrub-steppe. Its shape was carved from the rock by monumental floods during the ice ages leaving a rolling sandy landscape separated by steep cliffs carved from the native basalt.
